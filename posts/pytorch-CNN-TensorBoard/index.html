Supplementary Information

Normalization vs. Standardization

  • Ref: Preprocessing Data: Data Feature Normalization and Standardization

Normalization vs. Standardization: What‚Äôs the Difference?

  • Normalization: Scaling data proportionally to fit into a small specific range, such as [0, 1] or [-1, 1].
    • Formula: xi‚àímin(xi)max(xi)‚àímin(xi)\frac{x_i - min(x_i)}{max(x_i) - min(x_i)}max(xi‚Äã)‚àímin(xi‚Äã)xi‚Äã‚àímin(xi‚Äã)‚Äã
  • Standardization: Scaling data proportionally to fit into a distribution with a mean of 0 and a standard deviation of 1, so extreme values may not fall within [0, 1].
    • Formula: xi‚àíŒºsd(x)\frac{x_i - \mu}{sd(x)}sd(x)xi‚Äã‚àíŒº‚Äã

Common Standards for Both

  • Both techniques scale individual features (columns) and not the feature vectors of individual samples (rows).

Why Normalize Data?

  1. Improved Precision: Many machine learning algorithms are based on objective functions that assume all features have zero mean and the same order of magnitude for variances. If the variance of a feature is orders of magnitude larger than that of other features, it will dominate the learning algorithm and prevent it from learning correctly. Therefore, normalization is done to make different dimensions of features comparable, significantly improving the classifier‚Äôs accuracy.
  2. Faster Convergence: After normalization, the process of finding the optimal solution is noticeably smoother, making it easier to converge to the optimal solution.
